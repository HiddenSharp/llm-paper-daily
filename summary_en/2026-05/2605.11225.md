## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: LLM-based autonomous agents often generate plans that appear coherent but fail during execution due to infeasible actions, constraint violations, and compounding errors over long horizons. This "plan-execution misalignment" is a fundamental reliability gap.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: Current methods lack a trajectory-level optimization view. Diagnostic methods identify failures post-hoc without optimizing the plan; iterative refinement focuses on local steps or prompts; constraint-based methods apply localized fixes; and evolutionary methods search candidates without directly optimizing the loss induced by execution outcomes. There is a missing mechanism to treat the full trajectory as an optimizable object and convert execution feedback into update signals.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed PIVOT (Plan-Inspect-eVOlve Trajectories)**: A self-supervised framework that iteratively refines trajectories via environment interaction, treating them as optimizable objects.
- **Challenge 1: Systematic divergence between planning and execution with compounding errors**. The difficulty lies in plans failing in dynamic environments due to incorrect state assumptions or infeasible actions, where errors cascade. PIVOT addresses this by executing trajectories in the INSPECT stage to compute structured losses (with textual gradients encoding discrepancies) and using these signals in the EVOLVE stage to produce improved trajectories, directly optimizing against execution outcomes.
- **Challenge 2: Maintaining solution quality while ensuring computational efficiency**. The challenge is that traditional refinement methods are token-intensive and may suffer from quality fluctuations. PIVOT introduces a monotonic acceptance process to ensure non-decreasing solution quality and uses an efficient trajectory-level objective, requiring 3-5x fewer tokens than competing refinement methods.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation**: PIVOT consists of four stages: PLAN (generate candidate trajectories), INSPECT (execute and compute structured losses with textual gradients), EVOLVE (apply signals to produce improved trajectories), and VERIFY (final global constraint check). It supports both fully autonomous and Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) modes.
- **Evaluation Results**: Achieved state-of-the-art performance on DeepPlanning and GAIA benchmarks.
  - **HITL**: Established a strong upper bound with up to ~94% relative improvement in constraint satisfaction.
  - **Fully Autonomous**: Retained substantial gains, demonstrating the effectiveness of the core trajectory-refinement mechanism without external supervision.
- **Comparison**: Compared to other refinement methods, PIVOT is significantly more computationally efficient, requiring up to 3-5x fewer tokens, while effectively addressing the trajectory-level optimization gap left by prior work.

## 4. Conclusion
PIVOT demonstrates that feedback-based trajectory optimization (self-supervised or human-supervised) is a principled methodology for mitigating plan-execution gaps in autonomous agent systems. By treating the full trajectory as an optimizable object in a discrete language space and employing a monotonic acceptance mechanism, PIVOT achieves significant improvements in constraint satisfaction and task success rates while drastically reducing computational costs, offering a new paradigm for building reliable LLM agents.
