## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: Intensive Care Units (ICUs) generate long, dense, and evolving streams of clinical information. Physicians must repeatedly reassess patient states under time pressure, creating a clear need for reliable AI decision support systems.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: Existing ICU benchmarks typically treat historical clinician actions as ground truth. However, these actions are made under incomplete information and limited temporal context, making them potentially suboptimal and unsuitable for assessing the true reasoning capabilities of AI systems.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed RealICU Benchmark**: A hindsight-annotated benchmark for evaluating LLMs under realistic ICU conditions. Labels are created after senior physicians review the full patient trajectory, covering four tasks: Patient Status assessment, Acute Problems identification, Recommended Actions, and Red Flag actions.
- **Challenge 1: Suboptimal Ground Truth in Existing Benchmarks**. The difficulty lies in real-time doctor decisions being constrained by local information. The paper addresses this by introducing a "hindsight" annotation mechanism where experts refine labels based on the complete patient trajectory, providing a gold standard for ideal reasoning.
- **Challenge 2: Cognitive Biases in LLMs with Long-Context Data**. LLMs suffer from a "recall-safety tradeoff" and "anchoring bias" to early patient states. The paper exposes these failure modes through the RealICU-Gold and RealICU-Scale datasets and introduces ICU-Evo, a structured-memory agent, to study and mitigate these long-horizon reasoning issues.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Datasets**: Released RealICU-Gold (930 windows from 94 MIMIC-IV patients, human-annotated) and RealICU-Scale (11,862 windows extended by a physician-validated LLM Oracle).
- **Evaluation Results**: Existing LLMs, including memory-augmented ones, performed poorly on RealICU. Two major failure modes were identified: a trade-off between recall and safety in clinical recommendations, and an anchoring bias that prevents updating judgments as patient conditions evolve.
- **Comparison**: Unlike traditional behavior imitation benchmarks, RealICU evaluates whether AI possesses correct reasoning capabilities based on full context rather than blindly mimicking historical doctor actions. The introduced ICU-Evo agent improved long-horizon reasoning but did not fully eliminate safety failures, highlighting the benchmark's rigor.

## 4. Conclusion
RealICU provides a clinically grounded testbed for measuring and improving AI sequential decision-support in high-stakes care. By moving beyond simple behavior imitation and utilizing high-quality hindsight annotations, it reveals fundamental defects in current LLMs' understanding of long-context medical data, paving the way for more reliable and safe ICU AI co-pilots.
