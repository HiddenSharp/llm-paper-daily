## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: Reinforcement Learning (RL) is central to post-training LLM agents but offers only coarse trajectory-level rewards for long-horizon interactions. On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD) complements RL by providing dense token-level guidance from a teacher branch augmented with privileged context.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: Directly transferring OPSD to multi-turn agents is problematic due to compounding multi-turn instability that destabilizes supervision, and the need for asymmetric treatment of negative teacher rejections which may stem from imperfect skill retrieval rather than student errors. Naive combinations like GRPO+OPSD fail to address these issues effectively.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed SDAR (Self-Distilled Agentic Reinforcement Learning)**: A framework that treats OPSD as a gated auxiliary objective while maintaining RL as the primary optimization backbone.
- **Challenge 1: Multi-turn OPSD Instability**. As the student drifts from teacher-supported trajectories, token-level supervision becomes unreliable, causing surging KL divergence. SDAR addresses this by mapping detached token-level signals into a sigmoid gate, strengthening distillation only on teacher-endorsed positive-gap tokens.
- **Challenge 2: Asymmetric Trust in Privileged Guidance**. The teacher is not an independently stronger model but the same policy with privileged context; thus, its negative rejections may be noisy. SDAR softly attenuates negative teacher rejections, avoiding over-punishment for errors arising from imperfect skill retrieval.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Setup**: Evaluated on Qwen2.5 and Qwen3 model families across ALFWorld, WebShop, and Search-QA benchmarks.
- **Baselines**: Compared against GRPO, naive GRPO+OPSD, and other hybrid RL-OPSD baselines.
- **Results**:
  - SDAR substantially outperforms GRPO: +9.4% on ALFWorld, +7.0% on Search-QA, and +10.2% on WebShop-Acc.
  - Stability: SDAR avoids the instability observed in naive GRPO+OPSD.
  - Consistency: SDAR consistently outperforms hybrid RL-OPSD baselines across different model scales.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper addresses the sparsity of RL supervision and the instability of OPSD in multi-turn LLM agent training by proposing SDAR. By introducing a gating mechanism, SDAR effectively leverages dense token-level guidance from OPSD while handling noise from privileged contexts through asymmetric signal processing. Experiments demonstrate significant performance improvements and enhanced training stability across multiple benchmarks, establishing SDAR as an efficient paradigm for LLM agent post-training.
