## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: The field of Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) lacks a formal benchmark platform for "Strategic Coopetition" (mixed-motive scenarios involving both cooperation and competition). Existing research mostly focuses on purely cooperative or purely adversarial settings, failing to capture complex interdependence and complementarity found in real-world scenarios.
- **Why Existing Work Falls Short**: Current MARL benchmarks often lack formal theoretical grounding and fail to distinguish between "Payoff Structure" and "Reward Mechanism," making fine-grained motivational ablation studies difficult. Furthermore, they lack environments calibrated against historical cases, limiting their ability to validate algorithm performance in realistic coopetitive dynamics.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed**: Coopetition-Gym v1, a formally grounded benchmark platform for mixed-motive multi-agent reinforcement learning.
- **Challenge 1: How to formalize and decouple complex coopetitive motives?**
  - **Difficulty**: Traditional methods couple environmental payoffs with agent rewards, making it hard to independently analyze the impact of different motivational structures on strategies.
  - **Solution**: The paper designs a two-layer architecture that separates closed-form payoff structures from a parameterized reward layer (private, integrated, and cooperative modes). This enables "reward-type ablation," allowing precise control and analysis of motivational sources.
- **Challenge 2: How to ensure theoretical rigor and real-world relevance of environments?**
  - **Difficulty**: Synthetic environments often lack empirical basis, while real-world data is hard to standardize into RL environments, leading to a lack of ecological validity in benchmarks.
  - **Solution**: The platform includes 20 environments categorized into four mechanism classes (interdependence, trust/reputation, collective action, sequential interaction). Four environments are calibrated against historical cases (e.g., Samsung-Sony LCD alliance), achieving 81.7% to 98.3% match on validation rubrics, ensuring formal mechanisms capture empirically observed coopetitive dynamics.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation & Deployment**: The platform offers Gymnasium, PettingZoo Parallel, and PettingZoo AEC interfaces. It releases a suite of 126 reference algorithms (16 learning algorithms, 7 game-theoretic oracles, 2 heuristic baselines, 101 constant-action policies). Code is open-source (MIT), and datasets are released under CC-BY-4.0 with Croissant 1.0 metadata.
- **Evaluation Results**: A large-scale reference study trained 16 algorithms across all environments and reward configurations with 7 random seeds, producing a 25,708-run training corpus and a 1,116-run behavioral audit corpus. Methodological tools include statistical-gate discipline, critic learning-rate ablation, matrix-coverage verification, and continuous reliability diagnostics.
- **Comparison**: As the first platform to combine continuous-action mixed-motive environments, parameterized reward mutuality, calibrated interdependence coefficients, game-theoretic oracle baselines, and validated case studies, Coopetition-Gym v1 fills the gap in strategic coopetition within MARL benchmarks, offering richer evaluation dimensions than traditional pure cooperation/adversarial benchmarks.

## 4. Conclusion
Coopetition-Gym v1 provides a formally grounded benchmark platform for mixed-motive multi-agent reinforcement learning. By decoupling payoffs from rewards, calibrating against historical cases, and providing comprehensive reference algorithms and datasets, the platform enables systematic study of complex dynamics in strategic coopetition, advancing MARL from idealized scenarios to more realistic mixed-motive contexts.
