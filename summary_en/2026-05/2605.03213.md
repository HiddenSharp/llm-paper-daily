## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Large Language Models (LLMs) are evolving from isolated, stateless inference systems into Agentic AI systems that plan, invoke tools, maintain persistent memory, and collaborate via protocols like MCP and A2A. This shift introduces a materially different threat surface where agents accumulate sensitive context, hold credentials, and operate across pipelines not fully controlled by any single party.
- **Limitations of Existing Work**: Current defenses operate entirely within the software stack (e.g., prompt guards, sandboxes) and can be silently bypassed by privileged adversaries such as compromised cloud operators. These software-only measures fail to address threats like prompt injection, context exfiltration, and inter-agent message poisoning in distributed, multi-step agent workflows, as they do not alter the underlying hardware trust boundary.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed a**: Comprehensive survey framework synthesizing Confidential Computing (CC) for Agentic AI, bridging hardware trust primitives with agent-specific security requirements.
- **Challenge 1: Complex Agent Threat Surface vs. Ineffective Software Defenses**. The difficulty lies in the multi-layered nature of agents (perception, planning, memory, action, coordination) operating in untrusted distributed environments, where privileged software attacks cannot be mitigated by application-level controls. The paper addresses this by constructing an agent-centric threat model mapped to nine security goals and advocating for Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) to isolate code and data from privileged system software using hardware-rooted trust.
- **Challenge 2: Lack of Unified CC Architecture for Multi-hop Chains and Scale**. The challenge is the heterogeneity of TEE platforms (e.g., Intel SGX, AMD SEV-SNP) and the absence of end-to-end frameworks for compound attestation in multi-agent chains and performance optimization for GPU-TEEs at LLM scale. The paper provides a unified taxonomy of six TEE platforms, analyzing their deployment roles and tradeoffs, and distinguishes which CC defenses transfer from single-call inference versus those requiring new agentic designs.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation & Deployment Analysis**: Rather than proposing a new algorithm, the paper analyzes the deployment landscape of six major TEE platforms (Intel SGX, Intel TDX, AMD SEV-SNP, ARM TrustZone, ARM CCA, NVIDIA H100 CC). It evaluates their suitability for agent systems, emphasizing the role of remote attestation in establishing verifiable trust across distributed deployments.
- **Evaluation & Comparison**:
    - **Threat Model Comparison**: Unlike prior works focusing solely on model-level or single-inference security, this work establishes a holistic threat model covering the entire agent lifecycle.
    - **Defense Mechanism Comparison**: Contrasts pure software defenses (vulnerable to privileged attackers) with CC-based hardware defenses (providing strong isolation). It highlights that while hardware primitives are maturing, no coherent security substrate yet exists for production Agentic AI.
    - **Performance & Feasibility**: Analyzes performance tradeoffs of different TEEs, specifically identifying bottlenecks in GPU-TEE performance for large-scale LLM inference and the complexity of compound attestation for multi-hop agent interactions.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper systematically reviews the application of Confidential Computing in Agentic AI. The authors argue that as AI agents assume more autonomous tasks, traditional software-layer defenses are insufficient against privileged adversaries and complex interaction risks. By leveraging TEEs and remote attestation, CC offers hardware-level isolation and trust verification. However, significant gaps remain, particularly in creating mature end-to-end frameworks that handle multi-agent collaborative attestation and optimize performance for large-scale GPU-accelerated inference. The survey outlines six open challenges, providing a critical roadmap for building secure, production-grade agentic systems.
