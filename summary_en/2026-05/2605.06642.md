## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed as interactive agents, but optimizing them for long-horizon decision-making remains challenging. Current reinforcement learning (RL) methods are largely purely reactive, predicting actions solely based on the current state.
- **Limitations of Existing Work**: Purely reactive approaches lead to short-sighted exploration, unnecessary backtracking, and inconsistent behavior. The coupling of immediate action selection with long-term planning, combined with sparse and delayed rewards, complicates credit assignment and hinders effective exploration in long-horizon tasks.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed StraTA (Strategic Trajectory Abstraction)**: A simple framework that introduces an explicit trajectory-level strategy into agentic RL.
- **Challenge 1: Inefficient Credit Assignment and Exploration in Long-Horizon Tasks**. The difficulty lies in the hierarchical nature of decisions and delayed rewards, making it hard to attribute value to intermediate steps. StraTA addresses this by sampling a compact global strategy from the initial state and conditioning all subsequent actions on this strategy, decoupling high-level planning from low-level execution to provide consistent guidance.
- **Challenge 2: Lack of Strategy Diversity and Self-Correction**. Single strategies may get stuck in local optima. StraTA employs a hierarchical GRPO-style rollout design, enhanced by diverse strategy rollouts and critical self-judgment, jointly training strategy generation and action execution to improve robustness and sample efficiency.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Experimental Setup**: Evaluated on ALFWorld, WebShop, and SciWorld benchmarks.
- **Key Results**:
  - **ALFWorld**: Achieved a success rate of 93.1%.
  - **WebShop**: Achieved a success rate of 84.2%.
  - **SciWorld**: Attained an overall score of 63.5%.
- **Comparison**: StraTA consistently improves both sample efficiency and final performance over strong baselines. Notably, on SciWorld, it outperforms frontier closed-source models. Compared to traditional PPO or GRPO methods, StraTA's explicit strategy abstraction significantly enhances learning efficiency and behavioral consistency in long-horizon tasks.

## 4. Conclusion
StraTA addresses the challenges of short-sighted exploration and difficult credit assignment in reactive agents by introducing explicit trajectory-level strategy abstraction. By leveraging hierarchical training and diverse strategy mechanisms, it significantly boosts the performance and sample efficiency of LLM agents in complex interactive tasks, offering a new paradigm for building reliable long-horizon agents.
