## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Multi-agent LLM systems typically collaborate by exchanging natural-language messages. While simple and interpretable, this interface forces the sender's intermediate computations to be serialized into tokens, which the receiver must then reprocess.
- **Limitations of Existing Work**: This text-based communication incurs high generated-token costs, prefill overhead, and KV-cache memory usage. As the number of agents or communication rounds increases, this repetitive "write-read" cycle becomes a dominant cost bottleneck, inefficiently forcing model-to-model communication through a channel designed for humans.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed TFlow (Thought Flow)**: A weight-space communication framework for known and fixed receiver architectures. It compiles sender hidden states into transient, receiver-specific weight perturbations instead of text messages.
- **Challenge 1: How to avoid high prefill and KV-cache costs caused by serializing internal states?**
  - **Difficulty**: Traditional methods decode rich internal states into tokens, requiring the receiver to re-encode them, leading to inflated context lengths and computational redundancy.
  - **Solution**: TFlow uses a learned parameter generator to map sender agents' internal activations into low-rank LoRA perturbations targeting the receiver's modules. These perturbations are fused and applied only during generation, eliminating extra prefilling and avoiding context expansion.
- **Challenge 2: How to achieve instance-level adaptation without permanently altering model parameters?**
  - **Difficulty**: Direct weight updates compromise model generality and are costly; prompt-only methods fail to fully leverage deep semantic information from senders.
  - **Solution**: The generated LoRA perturbations are transient and applied only during inference for the current query, without permanently modifying model weights. This enables efficient instance-level adaptation while preserving the integrity of the base model.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation**: Experiments used three Qwen3-4B agents. Sender agents were frozen with role prompts, and a parameter generator converted activations into low-rank perturbations.
- **Evaluation Results**:
  - Across five benchmarks, TFlow improved accuracy by up to 8.5 points over a standalone receiver while reducing processed tokens by up to 32.69%.
  - **Comparison with Related Work**: Compared to a text-based three-agent baseline, TFlow reduced total processed tokens by up to 83.27% and wall-clock inference time by up to 4.6x.
  - It maintained competitive accuracy on four out of five benchmarks, demonstrating that transient low-rank weight perturbations serve as an efficient medium for multi-agent collaboration.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper identifies the efficiency bottlenecks of natural language-based communication in current multi-agent LLM systems and proposes TFlow. By transforming communication content into weight-space low-rank perturbations (LoRA), it significantly reduces computational and memory overhead. Experiments show that the method substantially decreases token consumption and inference latency while maintaining or improving accuracy, offering a new paradigm for efficient multi-agent collaboration.
