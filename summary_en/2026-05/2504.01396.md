## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: The exponential growth of AI-generated images (AIGIs) poses significant challenges to content authenticity and information security. Due to the continuous evolution of generative models, detectors require strong generalizability to handle unseen generators.
- **Why Existing Works Fail**: Traditional image classification relies on object-centric features. However, existing AIGI detectors suffer from "Few-Patch Bias," where models disproportionately rely on a minority of patches with conspicuous artifacts. This "Lazy Learner" effect causes detectors to neglect the uniform distribution of synthetic artifacts across all patches, limiting robustness and generalization.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed Panoptic Patch Learning (PPL) Framework**: Built on two principles: "All Patches Matter" (every patch contains artifacts) and "More Patches Better" (leveraging distributed artifacts improves robustness).
- **Challenge 1: Overcoming Few-Patch Bias caused by Lazy Learning**. The difficulty lies in models converging on obvious local artifacts while ignoring global cues. PPL addresses this via **Random Patch Replacement**, which randomly substitutes synthetic patches with real ones, compelling the model to identify artifacts in underutilized regions and encouraging broader patch usage.
- **Challenge 2: Ensuring Consistent Discriminative Capability Across All Patches**. The challenge is varying artifact strengths across patches. PPL introduces **Patch-wise Contrastive Learning** to enforce uniform discriminative capability across all patches, ensuring comprehensive utilization of forensic evidence and enhancing robustness.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Experimental Setup**: Evaluated on multiple benchmarks including GenImage, DRCT-2M, AIGCDetectionBenchmark, and the in-the-wild Chameleon dataset.
- **Results & Comparison**: PPL outperforms state-of-the-art methods in cross-dataset and cross-generator generalization tests. Attention map visualizations demonstrate that PPL achieves a more uniform attention distribution compared to naively trained models, confirming its ability to capture comprehensive artifacts.
- **Robustness**: Extensive experiments verify that leveraging distributed artifacts through PPL significantly enhances detection robustness and generalization across various settings.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper identifies the uniform distribution of artifacts in AIGIs and highlights the "Few-Patch Bias" in existing detectors due to lazy learning. The proposed PPL framework, utilizing random patch replacement and patch-wise contrastive learning, successfully forces models to uniformly utilize all patches. The method demonstrates superior generalization and robustness across multiple benchmarks, offering a new perspective and technical pathway for improving AI-generated image detection.
