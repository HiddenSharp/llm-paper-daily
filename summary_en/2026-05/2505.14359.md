## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: The rapid rise of AI-generated images (AIGIs) necessitates robust detection methods. However, existing detectors are often trained on biased datasets, leading to overfitting on spurious correlations with non-causal attributes (e.g., image format, size, or semantic content). This results in significant performance degradation when applied to unbiased or unseen datasets.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: A common mitigation strategy is dataset alignment via generative reconstruction to match semantic content. However, this paper argues that pixel-level alignment alone is insufficient. Reconstruction models tend to restore high-frequency details lost in real images (e.g., due to JPEG compression), creating a frequency-level misalignment where synthetic images exhibit richer high-frequency content. This inadvertently reinforces biased cues, causing models to associate high-frequency features with synthetic labels.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed Dual Data Alignment (DDA)**: A novel framework that aligns data in both pixel and frequency domains to eliminate reliance on biased features.
- **Challenge 1: Frequency Domain Misalignment**. Existing pixel-level alignment ignores spectral discrepancies, where reconstructed synthetic images retain excessive high-frequency details, creating new bias cues. **Solution**: DDA fuses real and synthetic image pairs in the frequency domain, forcing synthetic images to match the spectral distribution of real images, thereby removing spurious high-frequency correlations.
- **Challenge 2: Poor Generalization Across New Architectures**. Detectors struggle to generalize to emerging generative paradigms (e.g., visual auto-regressive generators). **Solution**: The authors introduce two new benchmarks: DDA-COCO (for testing on highly aligned data) and EvalGEN (featuring latest generative models). By training exclusively on DDA-aligned MSCOCO data, the detector achieves superior zero-shot generalization across diverse and new architectures.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Experimental Setup**: The detector was trained exclusively on DDA-aligned MSCOCO data without fine-tuning on specific generator datasets.
- **Results**: Evaluated on 11 benchmarks, including 4 in-the-wild datasets (Chameleon, WildRF, BFree-Online, SynthWildx) and 7 manually crafted ones. DDA consistently outperformed state-of-the-art methods across all benchmarks.
- **Performance Gain**: Achieved a +7.2% improvement on in-the-wild benchmarks, demonstrating significantly improved generalizability. The method showed robust performance against the latest visual auto-regressive generators in the EvalGEN set, where traditional methods failed.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper identifies that the poor generalization of AIGI detectors stems not only from pixel-level semantic bias but also from overlooked frequency-level misalignment. By proposing Dual Data Alignment (DDA) to correct distributions in both domains, the method effectively removes spurious correlations. Combined with the new DDA-COCO and EvalGEN benchmarks, extensive experiments prove that DDA significantly enhances detector robustness and generalization in diverse, unseen generative scenarios, offering a new pathway for building universal AIGI detection systems.
