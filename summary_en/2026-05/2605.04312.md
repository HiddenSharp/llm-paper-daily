## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: As AI agents become more capable and entrusted with decision-making, high-stakes multi-agent interactions involving cooperation, conflict, and persuasion are becoming common. However, our understanding of emergent behaviors and relative agent strengths in these dynamic contexts is limited.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: Static capability benchmarks suffer from two main issues:
    1. **Saturation**: Once models reliably solve a fixed set of tasks, the benchmark no longer tracks progress.
    2. **Contamination**: Benchmark tasks increasingly leak into training corpora over time, invalidating results.
    Static environments fail to capture dynamic interaction dynamics and cannot mitigate these risks.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed Agent Island**: A multiplayer simulation environment inspired by the TV show "Survivor," where LLM agents compete through inter-agent cooperation, conflict, and persuasion, creating a dynamic benchmark.
- **Challenge 1: Benchmark Saturation**
    - **Description & Difficulty**: In fixed task sets, scores plateau once models reach SOTA performance, making it impossible to distinguish superior capabilities.
    - **Solution**: Agent Island uses a "winner-take-all" mechanism where new models can always outperform current leaders. This dynamic competition prevents saturation and allows for continuous differentiation of model capabilities.
- **Challenge 2: Benchmark Contamination**
    - **Description & Difficulty**: Static tasks are prone to being included in training data, leading to memorization rather than genuine reasoning.
    - **Solution**: Agents compete against other adaptive agents rather than fixed tasks. The unpredictability of opponent behavior significantly reduces the risk of specific test cases being pre-trained on, thus resisting contamination.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation & Metrics**:
    - Players are ranked using a Bayesian Plackett-Luce model to quantify skill uncertainty.
    - Evaluated across 999 games involving 49 unique models.
- **Key Results**:
    - **openai/gpt-5.5** dominates with a posterior mean skill of 5.64.
    - The second (**openai/gpt-5.2**, 3.10) and third (**openai/gpt-5.3-codex**, 2.86) models are closely matched but significantly behind the leader.
- **Behavioral Insights**:
    - Game logs are released as a dataset.
    - Analysis reveals a "same-provider preference": models are 8.3 percentage points more likely to support a finalist from the same provider.
    - This bias varies by provider: strongest for OpenAI models, weakest for Anthropic models.
- **Comparison**: Unlike traditional static benchmarks (e.g., MMLU), Agent Island offers a dynamic, contamination-resistant evaluation framework that uncovers strategic interaction capabilities and social biases invisible to static tests.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper introduces Agent Island, a dynamic multi-agent game environment designed to address the saturation and contamination issues of static benchmarks. By having LLMs compete in a "Survivor"-style setting, it continuously evaluates strategic interaction capabilities. Experiments highlight GPT-5.5's dominance and reveal provider-specific biases. This work provides a new tool and perspective for tracking LLM progress in complex multi-agent scenarios.
