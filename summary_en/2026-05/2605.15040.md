## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Agentic modeling aims to transform LLMs into autonomous agents capable of solving complex tasks through planning, reasoning, tool use, and multi-turn interaction with environments.
- **Limitations of Existing Work**: Despite significant investment, open research is constrained by infrastructure and training gaps. High-performing systems often rely on proprietary codebases or services, while most open-source frameworks focus on orchestration and evaluation rather than scalable model training to enhance agentic capabilities.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed Orchard Framework**: An open-source framework for scalable agentic modeling, centered around "Orchard Env," a lightweight environment service providing reusable primitives for sandbox lifecycle management across diverse task domains and pipeline stages.
- **Challenge 1: Data Efficiency and Sparse Rewards in Agent Training**. Complex tasks like software engineering produce trajectories with many unproductive steps and sparse final rewards, making traditional RL difficult. **Solution**: The paper introduces "Credit-assignment SFT" to learn only from productive segments of unresolved trajectories and applies "Balanced Adaptive Rollout" for RL, significantly improving training efficiency and performance.
- **Challenge 2: Generality and Resource Cost Across Domains**. Building separate environments for different agent types (code, GUI, assistant) is resource-intensive and hinders reuse. **Solution**: Orchard Env acts as a thin, harness-agnostic layer enabling the reuse of data, training recipes, and evaluations. For instance, Orchard-GUI achieves strong performance with minimal data (0.4K distilled trajectories), demonstrating the architecture's efficient generality.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Orchard-SWE (Coding Agent)**: Built on Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking using 107K distilled trajectories. It achieves 64.3% on SWE-bench Verified after SFT and 67.5% after SFT+RL. This sets a new SOTA among open-source models of comparable size, outperforming OpenSWE-72B (66.0%) and Scale-SWE-Agent (64.0%).
- **Orchard-GUI (GUI Agent)**: A 4B vision-language agent trained with only 0.4K distilled trajectories and 2.2K open-ended tasks. It achieves success rates of 74.1%, 67.0%, and 64.0% on WebVoyager, Online-Mind2Web, and DeepShop respectively (68.4% average). It is the strongest open-source model, competitive with proprietary systems from OpenAI and Google, and outperforms GPT-4o (SoM).
- **Orchard-Claw (Personal Assistant)**: Trained with only 0.2K synthetic tasks, achieving 59.6% pass@3 on Claw-Eval, improving to 73.9% when paired with a stronger ZeroClaw harness.

## 4. Conclusion
Orchard addresses the fragmentation of infrastructure and lack of scalable training in agent research by providing a lightweight, open, and harness-agnostic environment layer. It demonstrates superior performance across coding, GUI, and personal assistant domains, proving that efficient data distillation and targeted training strategies enable small-parameter open-source models to match or exceed proprietary systems. The release of Orchard aims to accelerate innovation in the open-source AI community.
