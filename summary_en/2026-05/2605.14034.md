## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: The widespread application of LLM-based agents in social activities requires strong alignment with human social values, including self-cognition, dilemma decision-making, and self-emotions.
- **Why Existing Works Fall Short**: Current approaches, such as fine-tuning on society-isolated datasets or self-play alignment, suffer from poor generalization to unfamiliar cases. Due to the lack of high-quality social annotations and inefficient sampling, existing agents struggle to exhibit human-like emotions, appropriate morality, and correct decisions in social dilemmas.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed SoVA (Social Value Alignment)**: A novel framework that employs GraphRAG to convert abstract principles into value-based instructions, steering agent behavior by retrieving suitable instructions based on specific conversation contexts.
- **Challenge 1: Complexity of Social Contexts and Data Scarcity**. The difficulty lies in the nuanced, dynamic nature of social interactions and the bottleneck of high-quality annotated data. The paper addresses this by constructing a weak-supervision pipeline that auto-scales limited 'seed principles' via a Knowledge Graph, guided by descriptive psychological theories like Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs.
- **Challenge 2: Translating Descriptive Theories to Prescriptive Actions**. The challenge is converting abstract psychological concepts (e.g., Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions) into executable agent behaviors. The method encodes these theories as normative principles within GraphRAG, allowing the retrieval of context-specific instructions to guide expected behaviors and emotional responses.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation & Benchmark**: Experiments were conducted on the DAILYDILEMMAS benchmark, featuring binary-choice questions and open-ended conversations. Expected behaviors were defined using Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs and Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions.
- **Results**: SoVA demonstrated significant performance gains compared to prompt-based baselines.
- **Comparison**: The method outperformed baselines including ECoT, Plan-and-Solve, and Metacognitive prompting, showing higher ratios of expected behaviors in social dilemmas.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper introduces SoVA to address the lack of social value alignment in LLM agents. By integrating GraphRAG with established psychological theories, it transforms abstract values into retrievable, actionable instructions. This approach overcomes data scarcity and generalization issues, achieving superior performance over existing prompting methods on the DAILYDILEMMAS benchmark, thereby laying the groundwork for AI systems with emergent self-emotion and moral reasoning.
