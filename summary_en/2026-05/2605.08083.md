## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Test-time scaling (TTS) has emerged as a powerful paradigm for improving large language model (LLM) performance by allocating additional computation during inference.
- **Limitations of Existing Work**: Current TTS strategies are largely hand-crafted. Researchers manually design reasoning patterns and tune heuristics (e.g., when to branch, prune, or stop) based on intuition. This approach is inefficient and leaves a significant portion of the computation-allocation space unexplored, failing to achieve optimal accuracy-cost tradeoffs.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed AutoTTS**: An environment-driven framework that shifts the researcher's role from designing individual TTS heuristics to constructing environments where TTS strategies can be discovered automatically.
- **Challenge 1: Intractable Search Space and High Evaluation Cost**. Searching for optimal control policies via continuous LLM calls is prohibitively expensive and slow. **Solution**: The paper formulates width-depth TTS as controller synthesis over pre-collected reasoning trajectories and probe signals. Controllers decide actions (branch, continue, probe, prune, stop) and can be evaluated cheaply without repeated LLM calls.
- **Challenge 2: Difficult Search Convergence and Lack of Diagnostic Feedback**. Naive search struggles in complex control spaces, and failures are hard to diagnose. **Solution**: Introduces beta parameterization to make the search tractable and provides fine-grained execution trace feedback, enabling the agent to diagnose why a TTS program fails and improving discovery efficiency.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation**: AutoTTS utilizes an offline replay environment with 2D control and beta parameterization. An explorer LLM iteratively proposes candidate controllers, evaluates them, and refines proposals based on scaling curves and execution trace feedback.
- **Results**: Experiments on mathematical reasoning benchmarks demonstrate that the discovered strategies improve the overall accuracy-cost Pareto frontier compared to strong manually designed baselines (such as SC@64, ASC, ESC).
- **Generalization and Cost**: The discovered strategies generalize to held-out benchmarks and different model scales. The entire discovery process costs only $39.9 and takes 160 minutes, highlighting its affordability and efficiency.

## 4. Conclusion
AutoTTS revolutionizes TTS strategy development by automating the discovery process through environment construction rather than manual heuristic tuning. It effectively addresses the challenges of large search spaces and high evaluation costs, discovering strategies that outperform human-designed baselines at a minimal cost. The method demonstrates strong generalization across benchmarks and model sizes, offering a new paradigm for efficient inference-time computation allocation.
