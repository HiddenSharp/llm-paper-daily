## 1. What the Paper Did
- **Background**: As LLM agents tackle increasingly complex tasks, an ecosystem of execution-based benchmarks has emerged. Unlike static datasets, these benchmarks involve tightly coupled pipelines including natural language instructions, executable ground-truth programs, evaluation scripts, and containerized environments, making the measurement infrastructure itself susceptible to systemic logical flaws.
- **Why Existing Work Falls Short**: Current benchmark construction relies on domain experts but suffers from "solution fixation" and the "curse of knowledge," where creators anchor to their own implementations, leading to underspecified instructions and misaligned evaluation suites. Even human-validated benchmarks (e.g., SWE-bench Verified) contain residual noise. This noise is uniquely difficult to detect because correctness emerges from interactions among multiple artifacts, not just a single label, rendering traditional manual review insufficient.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed BenchGuard**: The first automated auditing framework for task-oriented, execution-based agent benchmarks, employing frontier LLMs as systematic auditors of the evaluation infrastructure.
- **Challenge 1: Detecting implicit errors arising from multi-component interactions**. Errors often stem from inconsistencies between instructions, ground-truth code, and scripts, which are hard to isolate. BenchGuard addresses this by cross-verifying all benchmark artifacts via structured LLM protocols, optionally incorporating agent solutions or execution traces as diagnostic evidence to systematically identify inconsistencies.
- **Challenge 2: High cost and blind spots of manual auditing**. Relying on domain experts is expensive and prone to missing defects caused by implicit assumptions. BenchGuard enables low-cost automated auditing; deployed on two scientific benchmarks, it identified defects missed by prior human reviews at a cost of under $15 for auditing 50 complex bioinformatics tasks.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Implementation & Deployment**: BenchGuard was deployed on two prominent scientific benchmarks: ScienceAgentBench and BIXBench.
- **Evaluation Results**:
    - Identified 12 author-confirmed issues in ScienceAgentBench, including fatal errors that rendered tasks unsolvable.
    - Achieved an exact match rate of 83.3% with expert-identified issues on the BIXBench Verified-50 subset.
    - Successfully caught defects that previous human reviews had entirely missed.
    - The full audit of 50 complex bioinformatics tasks cost under $15, demonstrating its practicality and value as a complement to human review.
- **Comparison**: Compared to methods relying solely on manual validation or static filtering (like the construction of SWE-bench Verified), BenchGuard detects deeper infrastructure logical errors and does so at a significantly lower cost than equivalent-scale manual expert audits.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper introduces BenchGuard, pioneering a paradigm where frontier LLMs serve not just as subjects of evaluation but as active participants in validating the evaluation infrastructure itself. By automating the cross-verification of benchmark components, BenchGuard effectively addresses pervasive implicit errors and noise in execution-based benchmarks. Experimental results show that the method not only replicates expert findings with high precision but also reveals critical defects overlooked by human reviewers at a minimal cost, offering a viable solution for AI-assisted benchmark development.
