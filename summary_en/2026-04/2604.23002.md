## 1. What the Paper Does
- **Background**: Formalizing informal scientific reasoning, particularly in physics (quantum mechanics and electromagnetism), into formally verifiable code (e.g., Lean4) poses significant challenges for Large Language Models (LLMs). Domain-specific notations (e.g., Dirac notation, vector calculus) add layers of complexity.
- **Why Existing Work Fails**: Current LLM and agentic approaches suffer from increased hallucination rates with task complexity or out-of-distribution shifts, leading to semantic and syntactic gaps. In natural sciences, semantic drift is magnified by incompatibilities between solvers and domain-specific notations/calculus, making robust autoformalization difficult.

## Core Contributions
- **Proposed FormalScience**: A domain-agnostic, human-in-the-loop agentic pipeline that enables single domain experts (without deep formal language experience) to produce syntactically correct and semantically aligned formal proofs at low economic cost.
- **Challenge 1: Difficulty in formalizing domain-specific symbols and complex semantics**. Physics-specific notations and abstractions hinder direct mapping to formal languages. The paper addresses this by employing a multi-stage agentic approach combined with human-in-the-loop feedback, effectively mitigating semantic loss during the conversion to Lean4 code.
- **Challenge 2: Semantic drift and verification trustworthiness in autoformalization**. It is unclear what is actually verified when full semantic preservation is unattainable. The paper provides the first systematic characterization of semantic drift in physics autoformalization, introducing concepts like "notational collapse" and "abstraction elevation" to reveal the actual verification guarantees when code compiles despite alignment drift.

## Implementation and Evaluation
- **Dataset Construction**: Applied FormalScience to construct FormalPhysics, a dataset of 200 university-level physics problems (primarily quantum mechanics and electromagnetism) with their Lean4 formal representations.
- **Evaluation Results**: Compared to existing formal math benchmarks, FormalPhysics achieves perfect formal validity and exhibits greater statement complexity.
- **Model Comparison**: The study evaluates open-source and proprietary models on statement autoformalization tasks using zero-shot prompting, self-refinement with error feedback, and a novel multi-stage agentic approach.
- **System Release**: The codebase and an interactive UI-based FormalScience system are released to facilitate autoformalization and theorem proving in scientific domains beyond physics.

## 4. Conclusion
This paper addresses the challenges of autoformalization in scientific domains by proposing the FormalScience human-in-the-loop agentic framework. By constructing the high-quality FormalPhysics dataset and systematically analyzing semantic drift, the work not only improves the accuracy and complexity handling of physics theorem formalization but also reveals the limitations of LLMs in scientific verification, marking a significant step towards explainable scientific verification.
